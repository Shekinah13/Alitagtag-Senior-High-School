History

The Philippine government sets a benchmark in 2016 with the start of full implementation of the senior high school program. Around 11,000 public and private schools started to cater the first batch of senior high school learners. This historic moment in education erased the record that the Philippines is the only country in Asia and one of only 3 countries worldwide with a 10-year academic cycle.

Despite of the negative feedbacks of the Senior High School implementation, which ushers in two additional years in basic education in the country, the Department of Education (DepEd) strongly pursued the opening on June 13, 2016 even there are critics who continue to doubt. The municipality of Alitagtag supported the implementation of the Senior High School program through the effort of the municipal mayor, Hon. Anthony Francis G. Andal, the district supervisor, Mrs. Conie C. Hernandez and the focal person of Alitagtag Senior High school, Mrs. Nalaine M. Quitain who is also the head of Alitagtag National High School during that time. Through the collaboration of the people involved in pursuing the senior high school in Alitagtag, the government granted the three 4 storey building with 36 classrooms. However, during the opening, only one building which was ready to use by the students.

On August 8, 2016 marked the blessing of the first building through Rev. Fr. Carlo M. Ilagan. The ribbon cutting was initiated by Congresswoman Ma. Theresa V. Collantes and the former focal person of Alitagtag National High School, Mrs. Nalaine M. Quitain.

ASHS first catered to 151 Grade 11 students. They were enrolled in Academic and TVL tracks. Particularly in Academic Track, there are 35 ABM students and 35 STEM students. Likewise, in TVL track, there are 28 HE students and 53 ICT students. The are eight pioneering teaching staff who are specialized in different subject areas. They were Dr. Wilhem D. Siscar (Master Teacher II) major in Science; Mr. Pablo A. Amurao, Jr. (Teacher III) major in Social Studies; Mr. Ryan R. Ramos major in English; Mrs. Marites H. Lalogo (Teacher III) major in Mathematics; Mr. Jaypee D. Pedrigal (Teacher II) major in ICT; Mr. Reginald M. Valencia (Teacher II) major in Physical Education; Mr. John Vincent M. Estremadura (Teacher II) major in Accounting and Miss Gellian Mae P. Panganiban major in Home Economics under the supervision of Mrs. Nalaine M. Quitain for the first two months of its implementation due to school heads’ transition. In the same day of the blessing, Mrs. Quitain turned over the supervision of Alitagtag Senior High School to the new principal of Alitagtag National High School, Mrs. Emily A. Mallari. It was August 8, 2016, when Mrs. Mallari formally appointed as the principal of ASHS.

The first few months was a struggle but through the collaboration and hardwork of all the persons looking forward for the success of its implementation they have created a cohesive environment of teamwork where everyone works hard, everyone is respected, and everyone can succeed.

Courses Offered

Content:

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics)

ABM (Accountancy, Business, Management)

HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences)

TVL (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood)

What is STEM?

Content

This strand is designed for students who desire to pursue college degrees centered on Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM). This strand provides preparation for students in using science, math, engineering, and technology to solve real-life problems and advance national development.

What is ABM?

Content

The ABM Strand is for those who plan to take up business-related courses in higher education or engage in business, entrepreneurship, and other business-related careers. The Accountancy, Business and Management (ABM) strand would focus on the basic concepts of financial management, business management, corporate operations, and all things that are accounted for. ABM can also lead you to careers on management and accounting which could be sales manager, human resources, marketing director, project officer, bookkeeper, accounting clerk, internal auditor, and a lot more.

What is HUMSS?

Content

Humanities and Social Sciences is a strand offered to senior high school students under the Academics track. The HUMSS Senior High School strand is designed for students who intend to take up journalism, communication arts, liberal arts, education, and other social-science related courses in college. Because these courses revolve heavily around interacting with other people, the Senior High School HUMSS strand is all about improving students’ reading, writing, and speaking skills.

What is TVL?

Content

The Technical-Vocational-Livelihood track is designed to prepare senior high school students for employment after graduation. These vocational courses help students all over the Philippines:
-Provide preparation for occupation of future OFWs abroad
-Gain the experience and skills they need to earn their Certificates of Competency and National Certifications, which they will need to seek future employment
-Enter college with job-ready skills, especially for courses related to their TVL track
